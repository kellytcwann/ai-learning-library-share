# Insurance POS Basics

Insurance POS is the front-office sales platform that turns a customer conversation into needs analysis, quote/illustration, application capture, document submission, and case tracking.

## Why It Matters

POS is the entry point of the insurance sales journey. Poor POS design can create duplicate data entry, quote explanation issues, missing documents, NIGO, manual referrals, slow product launches, and weaker customer experience.

## Typical Journey

| Step | Example | What To Observe |
|---|---|---|
| Needs analysis | Capture protection, savings, health needs | Is the process guided and auditable? |
| Product selection | Select base plan and riders | Are rules system-driven or manually interpreted? |
| Illustration | Generate premium / benefits projection | Is it easy to explain? |
| Application | Capture proposal / e-application data | Is data reused or rekeyed? |
| Document upload | Upload KYC, medical, financial documents | Are documents checked before submission? |
| Submission | Send to underwriting / operations | What causes NIGO or referral? |

## Key Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| FNA / needs analysis | Customer needs analysis supporting suitability and recommendation rationale. |
| Illustration | Premium, benefit, cash value, or projection output. |
| Rider | Optional add-on benefit. |
| Suitability | Whether the product fits the customer's needs and circumstances. |
| NIGO | Not in good order; application is incomplete or incorrect. |
| STP | Straight-through processing without manual rework. |
| Referral | Manual review due to rules, risk, or exceptions. |

## BA / PM Relevance

| Lens | What To Do |
|---|---|
| BA | Map journey, fields, rules, pain points, and controls. |
| PM | Clarify scope, stakeholders, timeline, outputs, and dependencies. |
| FDE | Understand where data is created, how it moves, and what can be automated. |

## Questions To Ask

- Which steps are performed inside the current POS / sales platform?
- Which steps happen outside it, such as email, Excel, CRM, or manual review?
- What are the top reasons for NIGO or referral?
- Which product rules are validated before submission?
- What evidence do we have: screenshots, process map, case logs, user feedback?

## Meeting-Ready Wording

> “Can I confirm whether the current POS / sales platform scope covers quote, illustration, application capture, document upload, and submission, or only part of that journey?”
